# Weekly Review

## Overview
Without deliberate review, weeks blur into each other. The weekly review is a 30-min ritual that turns time into learning. Done consistently, it's one of the highest-leverage productivity practices there is.

## When to Use
- End of the work week (Friday afternoon is typical)
- Before planning next week
- After a big launch or milestone
- When feeling like "where did this week go?"
- Onboarding a new project — to establish rhythm

## Process

### 1. Collect the data — 5 min
Pull the raw inputs:
- Calendar: what meetings happened?
- Task list: what moved to done?
- Commits / docs / emails: what was produced?
- Messages / Slack: what conversations happened?

Don't judge yet — just collect.

### 2. The 5 questions — 15 min
Answer briefly (one sentence each):

**A. What shipped?**
Concrete output: decisions made, code merged, docs finished, deals closed.

**B. What didn't ship, and why?**
Be honest. "Underestimated effort" / "Got pulled into meetings" / "Depended on someone who didn't deliver".

**C. What did I learn?**
One thing — about the work, about the team, about myself. If nothing, the week was wasted.

**D. What drained me?**
Activities that cost more energy than they were worth. Candidates to cut or delegate.

**E. What energized me?**
Activities that paid back more than they took. Candidates to do more of.

### 3. Trace the gaps
For items that didn't ship:
- Was the estimate wrong (too optimistic)?
- Was I unblocked but didn't start? (motivation/priority)
- Was I blocked but didn't escalate? (communication)
- Did scope creep? (saying no problem)

Patterns matter more than single instances. If "got pulled into meetings" appears 3 weeks running, it's not a one-off.

### 4. Name 3 things for next week
Not a full plan — just the 3 priorities:
- 1 big rock (the thing that, if done, makes the week a win)
- 1 important but not urgent (investment in future weeks)
- 1 thing to STOP or delegate

### 5. Close the open loops
Before Friday EOD:
- Reply to all emails > 24h old (even if just "I'll get to this Monday")
- Clear Slack DMs with "read & acknowledge"
- Update anyone waiting on you with status
- Write tomorrow's / Monday's "one big thing"

The weekend is cleaner when the week closes cleanly.

### 6. (Monthly, extended) — the pattern review
Once a month, look across the last 4 weekly reviews:
- What keeps appearing in "drained me"?
- What's the honest pattern in "didn't ship"?
- Is my calendar drifting away from what I said mattered?

Monthly pattern reviews catch drift that weekly reviews miss.

## Rationalizations

| Excuse | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| "I don't have 30 minutes on Friday" | You have 30 hours of work. You have 30 minutes for review |
| "I already know how the week went" | You know the story you're telling yourself. Data will surprise you |
| "I'll do it Monday morning" | Monday is for forward motion; Friday is for reflection |
| "There's nothing to review this week" | Then write "no notable progress". That's also data |
| "I need a fancy template" | 5 questions on a blank page works fine |

## Red Flags
- Skipped 3+ weeks in a row
- Weekly review consistently shows "blocked by meetings" with no action
- Every week's "one big thing" is different and forgotten
- Review becomes a guilt session instead of a learning session
- No record — writing it down is what makes it stick
- Review covers work only, not energy / mood / relationships

## Verification
- [ ] 30 min scheduled weekly, recurring
- [ ] All 5 questions answered (shipped / didn't ship / learned / drained / energized)
- [ ] 3 priorities named for next week
- [ ] Open loops closed before weekend
- [ ] Written down somewhere persistent (not in head)
- [ ] Monthly pattern review done (every 4 weeks)
